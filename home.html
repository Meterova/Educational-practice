    • Information

    Body mass index is a formula used to assess a person’s body weight relative to height (National Heart, Lung, and Blood Institute, 1998).

    BMI – body mass index-reflects a person's state of health. Using the body mass index, you can find out whether you are overweight, or, conversely, whether you are underweight. According to research from the Johns Hopkins University School of Hygiene and Public health, BMI is a representative indicator of body fat that depends on age, gender, and not ethnicity (The Johns Hopkins University School of Hygiene and Public Health, 1996).

    BMI takes into account natural variations in body shape, giving a healthy weight range for a particular height. As well as measuring your BMI, healthcare professionals may take other factors into account when assessing if you're a healthy weight.

    For many people, the body mass index is far from optimal. The body mass index for women is considered normal if the indicator is in the range from 20 to 22. For men, from 23 to 25.

    BMI is not used for muscle builders, long distance athletes, pregnant women, the elderly or young children. This is because BMI does not take into account whether the weight is carried as muscle or fat, just the number. Those with a higher muscle mass, such as athletes, may have a high BMI but not be at greater health risk. Those with a lower muscle mass, such as children who have not completed their growth or the elderly who may be losing some muscle mass may have a lower BMI. During pregnancy and lactation, a woman's body composition changes, so using BMI is not appropriate.

    BMI = W / H2 (W – body weight in kilograms, H-height in meters)
